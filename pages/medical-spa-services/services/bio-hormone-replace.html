Early Hormone Discoveries (Late 1800s–1930s) Scientists in the late 19th century began realizing glands like the ovaries, testes, thyroid, and adrenal glands released chemical messengers into the body. This eventually led to the field of endocrinology. A major breakthrough came in the early 1900s when researchers isolated hormones such as:

  • Estrogen
  • Progesterone
  • Testosterone

In the 1920s and 1930s, scientists learned how to extract and purify hormones from animal organs and urine. Pharmaceutical companies quickly saw medical potential.
